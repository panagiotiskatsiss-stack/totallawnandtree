Emergency Tree Services When You Need Them

Tree emergencies don't wait for business hours. When a storm brings down a tree on your home, a large limb threatens your family's safety, or you're dealing with any urgent tree situation, Total Tree Service is ready to respond 24 hours a day, 7 days a week.

Our emergency crews are equipped to handle the most challenging situations safely and efficiently. We understand the stress and danger that fallen trees and storm damage create, and we work quickly to secure your property and restore your peace of mind.

Emergency Situations We Handle

  • Storm Damage: Trees and limbs downed by high winds, ice, or severe weather.
  • Trees on Structures: Fallen trees on homes, garages, vehicles, or other buildings.
  • Blocking Access: Trees blocking driveways, roads, or emergency access routes.
  • Hanging Limbs: Dangerous branches that could fall at any moment.
  • Leaning Trees: Trees threatening to fall due to root damage or soil erosion.
  • Power Line Hazards: Trees or limbs near or on power lines (we coordinate with utility companies).
  • Uprooted Trees: Trees with exposed root systems that pose falling risks.

Frequently Asked Questions

How quickly can you respond to emergencies? +
We typically respond within 1-4 hours for true emergencies, depending on our current call volume and location. During major storm events when call volume is high, we prioritize based on danger level and work through calls as quickly as possible.
Is emergency tree service more expensive? +
Emergency services typically cost more than scheduled work due to the immediate response required and often more hazardous conditions. However, we always provide upfront pricing before starting work so there are no surprises.
Will my insurance cover emergency tree removal? +
In many cases, yes. If a tree damages your home or other insured structure, tree removal is often covered. We can provide documentation and work with your insurance company to help with your claim.
What if a tree is on power lines? +
Never approach trees that are touching or near power lines. Call us and your utility company immediately. We coordinate with utility crews to ensure power is safely disconnected before we work near any lines.

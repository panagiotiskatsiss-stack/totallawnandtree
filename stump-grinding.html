Professional Stump Grinding Services

After a tree is removed, the remaining stump can be an eyesore and a nuisance. Total Tree Service provides fast, efficient stump grinding services throughout Washington, NJ and the surrounding areas. Our powerful stump grinders can handle stumps of any size, grinding them down well below ground level so you can use your yard space however you like.

How Stump Grinding Works

Stump grinding uses a specialized machine with a rotating cutting wheel to chip away the wood. Here's what to expect:

  • Grinding Depth: We typically grind stumps 6-12 inches below ground level, though we can go deeper if needed for construction.
  • Wood Chips: The grinding process produces wood chips and mulch. You can keep these for your garden or we can haul them away.
  • Root System: Grinding removes the stump but leaves roots in place. They will decay naturally over time.
  • Quick Process: Most stumps can be ground in 30 minutes to 2 hours depending on size.

What Happens After Grinding?

After we finish grinding, you'll have a hole filled with wood chips and mulch. Options for this area include:

  • Leave the mulch to decompose and settle naturally
  • Add topsoil and plant grass seed
  • Plant a new tree or shrub
  • Install a garden bed or landscaping feature
  • We can haul away the chips and fill with soil if preferred

Stump Grinding vs. Stump Removal

Understanding your options

Factor Stump Grinding Full Stump Removal
Process Grind stump 6-12" below ground Dig out entire stump and roots
Speed Fast (30 min - 2 hours) Slower (several hours)
Cost More affordable More expensive
Yard Impact Minimal disruption Large hole left behind
Root System Roots left to decay naturally Roots removed completely
Best For Most residential situations Construction requiring full root removal

For most homeowners, stump grinding is the preferred choice due to lower cost and minimal yard disruption.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does stump grinding cost? +
Stump grinding is typically priced by stump diameter. Expect $75-$150 for small stumps (under 12"), $150-$300 for medium stumps (12-24"), and $300-$500+ for large stumps (24"+). We offer discounts for multiple stumps. Contact us for a free, accurate estimate.
How deep do you grind? +
Standard grinding is 6-12 inches below ground level, which is sufficient for planting grass or most landscaping. If you're planning construction or need deeper grinding, just let us know and we can accommodate.
What about the roots? +
Stump grinding removes the stump but leaves the root system in place. Roots will naturally decay over time (typically 5-10 years). They won't regrow without the stump. If full root removal is needed, we can discuss complete stump removal options.
Can I plant a new tree where the stump was? +
Yes! After grinding, we recommend removing as much of the wood chips as possible, adding fresh topsoil, and waiting a few months before planting. This allows the remaining wood chips to begin decomposing.

Professional Tree Pruning Services

Proper pruning is essential for maintaining healthy, beautiful, and safe trees. At Total Tree Service, our certified arborists understand the science behind tree pruning and use industry-best practices to enhance your trees' structure, health, and appearance.

Unlike topping or improper trimming that can damage trees, professional pruning promotes healthy growth, removes hazards, and improves the overall aesthetics of your landscape. We follow ANSI A300 standards for tree care to ensure every cut benefits your tree.

Benefits of Professional Tree Pruning

  • Improved Tree Health: Removing dead, diseased, or crossing branches prevents decay and promotes vigorous growth.
  • Enhanced Safety: Eliminating weak or overhanging branches reduces the risk of property damage and personal injury.
  • Better Appearance: Proper shaping improves your tree's natural form and your property's curb appeal.
  • Increased Sunlight: Crown thinning allows more light to reach your lawn and garden below.
  • Storm Resistance: Well-pruned trees are more resistant to wind damage during severe weather.
  • Fruit Production: Proper pruning of fruit trees improves fruit quality and yield.

When to Prune Your Trees

The best time for pruning depends on the tree species and your goals:

  • Late Winter/Early Spring: Ideal for most deciduous trees while they're dormant.
  • After Flowering: Best for spring-flowering trees like dogwood and magnolia.
  • Any Time: Dead, damaged, or hazardous branches should be removed as soon as noticed.
  • Avoid: Pruning during active growth in spring or during fall when trees are preparing for dormancy.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about tree pruning

When is the best time to prune trees? +
Most trees are best pruned during late winter or early spring while dormant, just before new growth begins. This timing minimizes stress and allows the tree to heal quickly. However, dead or hazardous branches can and should be removed any time of year for safety. Spring-flowering trees are best pruned right after they finish blooming.
How often should trees be pruned? +
Most mature trees benefit from pruning every 3-5 years to maintain health and structure. Younger trees may need more frequent attention to develop proper form. Fast-growing species or trees near structures may need annual maintenance. We can assess your trees and recommend an appropriate schedule.
How much does tree pruning cost? +
Pruning costs vary based on tree size, species, condition, and accessibility. Small trees typically cost $100-$300, medium trees $300-$600, and large trees $600-$1,000+. We provide free estimates so you know exactly what to expect before any work begins.
What's the difference between pruning and trimming? +
The terms are often used interchangeably, but technically pruning refers to removing specific branches for tree health and structure, while trimming typically refers to cutting for aesthetic shaping. Professional arborists focus on proper pruning techniques that benefit the tree's long-term health.

Expert Tree Removal Services

At Total Tree Service, we specialize in safe and efficient tree removal for residential and commercial properties throughout Washington, NJ and the surrounding areas. Whether you have a dead tree that's become a safety hazard, a storm-damaged tree threatening your home, or simply need to clear space for a new project, our experienced team has the skills and equipment to get the job done right.

When Do You Need Tree Removal?

Not every tree problem requires removal, but there are situations when it's the best‚Äîor only‚Äîoption:

  • Dead or Dying Trees: Trees that are dead, diseased beyond treatment, or in severe decline pose falling risks and should be removed promptly.
  • Storm Damage: Trees with major structural damage from storms, including split trunks or large broken limbs, may be unsafe to save.
  • Hazardous Location: Trees growing too close to power lines, buildings, or other structures may need removal to prevent damage.
  • Root Problems: Trees with roots damaging foundations, driveways, or underground utilities.
  • Construction or Landscaping: Trees that need to be cleared for building projects or landscape redesigns.
  • Overcrowding: Removing select trees to improve the health and appearance of remaining trees.

Our Tree Removal Process

Frequently Asked Questions About Tree Removal

Everything you need to know about our professional tree removal services in Washington, NJ and surrounding areas

How much does tree removal cost in Washington, NJ? +

Tree removal costs depend on the size, location, and complexity of the job. Here's a general pricing guide:

  • Small trees (under 25 feet): $500 - $1,000
  • Medium trees (25-50 feet): $1,000 - $3,000
  • Large trees (over 50 feet): $3,000 and up

Several factors can affect the final price including proximity to structures or power lines, the tree's condition (dead, diseased, or leaning), accessibility for equipment, the number of branches and trunk diameter, and whether stump grinding is included. We provide free on-site estimates so you know exactly what to expect before any work begins.

Do I need a permit to remove a tree in New Jersey? +

Permit requirements vary by municipality throughout New Jersey. Many towns in Warren, Hunterdon, and Morris counties require permits for trees over a certain diameter (typically 6-8 inches DBH - diameter at breast height). Some municipalities have protected species ordinances or heritage tree programs that require additional approval.

We handle all the permit paperwork for you as part of our service. During your free estimate, we'll advise whether a permit is needed and include any permit fees in your quote. This takes the hassle out of the process and ensures full compliance with local regulations.

How long does tree removal take? +

The duration depends on the tree's size and the complexity of the removal:

  • Small trees: 1-2 hours
  • Medium trees: 2-4 hours
  • Large trees: 4-8 hours (full day)
  • Very large or hazardous trees: May require multiple days

Trees near power lines, buildings, or in tight spaces take longer due to the extra care required. We always prioritize safety over speed. During your consultation, we'll give you an accurate time estimate for your specific situation.

What happens to the wood and debris after removal? +

We offer several options for handling the wood and debris:

  • Complete removal: We haul away all wood, branches, and debris, leaving your property clean
  • Wood chips/mulch: We can chip the branches on-site and leave the mulch for your garden beds
  • Firewood: We can cut the trunk into firewood-length rounds and stack them for you
  • Log sections: If you have a specific use for the wood (milling, crafts), we can cut it to your specifications

Just let us know your preference when scheduling, and we'll accommodate your needs. Complete debris removal is included in most quotes.

Do you remove the stump too? +

Stump grinding is available as an additional service and is highly recommended. Most customers choose to have the stump ground 6-12 inches below ground level, which allows you to plant grass, install landscaping, or even plant a new tree in its place.

Stump grinding typically adds $100-$400 to the total cost depending on the stump's size and root system. We offer a discount when stump grinding is combined with tree removal since our equipment is already on-site. We'll include stump grinding pricing in your estimate so you can decide.

Is tree removal covered by homeowners insurance? +

In many cases, yes! Homeowners insurance often covers tree removal when:

  • A tree falls on your home, garage, fence, or other covered structure
  • Storm damage causes a tree to become hazardous
  • A fallen tree blocks your driveway or accessibility

Coverage typically doesn't include removing healthy trees for landscaping purposes or preventive removal (though some policies have exceptions). We recommend contacting your insurance company before scheduling. We can provide detailed invoices and documentation to support your claim, and we're happy to work directly with your insurance adjuster if needed.

What is the best time of year to remove a tree? +

Trees can be safely removed year-round, but some seasons offer advantages:

  • Winter (Dec-Feb): Often the best time - trees are dormant, there's less foliage to manage, and frozen ground can support heavy equipment better. Also typically lower demand means faster scheduling.
  • Late Fall (Oct-Nov): Good visibility after leaves drop, easier to assess the tree's structure
  • Spring/Summer: Fine for removal, though more foliage means more cleanup. We avoid nesting season for protected bird species.

For hazardous or dead trees, don't wait - these should be removed as soon as possible regardless of season to prevent property damage or injury.

How do I know if a tree needs to be removed? +

Several signs indicate a tree may need removal:

  • Dead or dying: No leaves in growing season, brittle branches, peeling bark
  • Significant lean: Especially if the lean has recently increased
  • Trunk damage: Large cavities, cracks, or decay in the main trunk
  • Root problems: Visible root rot, heaving soil, or roots damaging foundations
  • Storm damage: Major limb loss, split trunk, or hanging branches
  • Disease or pest infestation: Widespread fungal growth, boring insects, or disease symptoms
  • Proximity issues: Growing into power lines, foundation, or other structures

If you're unsure, we offer free assessments. Our certified arborists can evaluate your tree's health and recommend the best course of action - which might be pruning, treatment, or removal.

Can you remove a tree close to my house or power lines? +

Yes, we specialize in difficult and technical removals. Our team is trained and equipped to safely remove trees in challenging locations including:

  • Trees touching or near your home, garage, or shed
  • Trees entangled with power lines (we coordinate with utility companies)
  • Trees in tight spaces between buildings
  • Trees on steep slopes or difficult terrain
  • Trees over pools, patios, or delicate landscaping

For trees near power lines, we follow strict safety protocols and may coordinate with JCP&L or your local utility company. These jobs require specialized rigging techniques where we lower sections carefully rather than letting them fall. This takes more time but ensures zero damage to your property.

What's the difference between tree removal and tree trimming? +

Tree removal means taking down the entire tree, while trimming (or pruning) involves selectively removing branches to improve the tree's health, safety, or appearance while keeping the tree alive.

Choose removal when: The tree is dead, dying, diseased beyond treatment, structurally unsafe, or causing unavoidable problems with structures/utilities.

Choose trimming when: The tree is healthy but needs shaping, has some dead branches, is growing into structures, or needs crown thinning for better light/air circulation.

During your free consultation, we'll honestly assess whether your tree needs full removal or if pruning could address your concerns. We never recommend removal when trimming would suffice.

How do you ensure safety during tree removal? +

Safety is our top priority on every job. Our comprehensive safety approach includes:

  • Site assessment: Evaluating hazards, drop zones, and obstacles before work begins
  • Proper equipment: Well-maintained chainsaws, rigging systems, and safety gear
  • Trained crew: All team members are trained in OSHA safety standards and tree care best practices
  • Clear communication: Continuous communication between ground crew and climbers
  • Controlled removal: Using ropes and rigging to lower heavy sections rather than letting them free-fall
  • Property protection: Laying down plywood or tarps to protect lawns, driveways, and landscaping

We also ask that homeowners keep children, pets, and bystanders away from the work zone during removal for everyone's safety.
